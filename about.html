The Torchbearer of Inclusive India

Rajeev Bhatt is one of India’s most respected pioneers in the field of inclusive education and learning disabilities. With a journey spanning over three decades, he has dedicated his life to ensuring that children with autism, dyslexia, ADHD, and other learning differences are not defined by limitations—but empowered by opportunity.

Honoured with the National Award for Empowerment of Persons with Disabilities, presented by the Hon’ble President of India Droupadi Murmu, Rajeev Bhatt’s work represents a transformational shift in how India understands neurodiversity, education, and dignity.

Philosophy & Approach

Rajeev Bhatt’s work focuses on potential, not deficits. His inclusive framework emphasises:

Strength-based learning tailored to individual abilities

Social, emotional, and life skills training

Dignity, independence, and self-belief

Hands-on skill development and vocational readiness

Adhyayan Inclusive Learning Centre: A Model of Hope

As the Director of Adhyayan Inclusive Learning Centre, Rajeev Bhatt developed one of India’s most impactful IEP-based (Individualised Education Program) models. The centre is built on strength-based learning—where every child’s abilities, interests, and pace guide their educational journey.

At Adhyayan, children are supported not just academically, but holistically—through emotional development, social skills, independence training, and real-world readiness. Today, many students from the centre are successfully working across hospitality, culinary arts, creative media, digital design, sports, fitness training, and professional music production—leading independent, meaningful lives.

Their success stories stand as living proof of what inclusive environments can achieve.

Over the last 30 years, Rajeev Bhatt has travelled extensively across India—reaching metros, small towns, and underserved communities alike. Mission

To create an inclusive India where every child—regardless of neurotype—has access to dignity, opportunity, and meaningful participation in society.

Rajeev Bhatt’s mission is to replace stigma with understanding, judgement with compassion, and exclusion with empowerment—by building systems that truly work for neurodiverse learners.

Vision

An India where learning differences are recognised as diversity, not disability—and where every child is supported to thrive, contribute, and lead with confidence.

He envisions inclusive education not as a separate system, but as the foundation of a just and progressive society.
